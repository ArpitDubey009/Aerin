I. Form

An impossible knot,
cast in optical glass.

The geometry traces a single continuous loop — no seams, no joins. What looks unstable is mathematically balanced: a torus knot, the same family of curve that governs orbital resonance.

II. Material

Transmission, not reflection.

Light doesn't bounce off this surface — it passes through it, bending and refracting based on the room's own illumination. The brass ring at its core never changes; only what surrounds it does.
